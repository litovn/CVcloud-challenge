  • On the optimization of Software Obfuscation against Hardware Trojans in Microprocessors

    IEEE pubblication   |   Paper here

    The quest for low production cost and short time-to-market, as well as the complexity of modern integrated circuits, pushed towards globalization of the supply chain of silicon devices. Such a production paradigm raised several security threats among which Hardware Trojan Horses (HTHs), which became a serious issue not only for academics but also for industry in the very last years. Indeed, it has been demonstrated that HTHs can be inserted into microprocessors allowing the attacker to run malicious software, acquire root privileges, and steal secret user information.
    We present the use of software obfuscation to protect systems against HTHs that aim at stealing information from the microprocessor while it is executing a program. Moreover, we present a Genetic Algorithm-based approach to optimize the application of the anti-HTH methodology by maximizing the obtained obfuscation while minimizing the introduced overhead.
    We proved the effectiveness and efficiency of the proposed methodology on the Ariane 64bit RISC-V microprocessor running a set of MiBench benchmarks and cryptographic programs.
